About Me

Hi! I'm currently doing research at Gehrke Lab in MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology), as part of the Master's in Bioengineering program from IQS. I'm using synthetic biology to develop a point-of-care rapid diagnostic test to address emerging viral outbreaks.
I believe the exchange of ideas between science and society is essential to the advancement of society through research: science should adress social needs and society should pay attention to the possibilities science offers.
This is why I'm commited to science outreach by posting comments about recent biotechnology breakthroughs in ASBTEC's social networks.
This blog is focused on explaining how synthetic biology is reinventing nature (including ourselves) and its social and medical implications, using a lenguage that everybody understands while being true to the facts. Occasionally I will also share some professional and personal experiences that have shaped my point of view.
