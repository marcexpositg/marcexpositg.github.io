Translating synthetic biology advances into health solutions

I am a biological engineer interested in advancing healthcare by understanding and designing biology. My scientist-engineer approach provides me a valuable perspective to bring research solutions to the real world. For instance, during my stay at MIT with Prof. Lee Gehrke, I improved current surveillance strategies for emerging viruses such as Zika or Dengue by reducing the development time and cost of paper-based diagnostic tests. Before that, I started and led a team of 12 students to develop a personalized gluten sensor for celiac patients, which was awarded with the Bronze Medal in the 2018 iGEM competition. Currently, I am a master’s student solving health problems by combining synthetic biology and bioinformatics in the laboratory of Prof. Marc Güell. Being awarded with the “La Caixa” fellowship to develop my PhD studies in the US, I look forward to strengthening my skills in bioengineering with the objective of improving healthcare through research.
